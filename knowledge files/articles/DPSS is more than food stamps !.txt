Most county DPSS offices in California offer far more than just “food stamps.” Through DPSS and other state programs, you may qualify for food benefits, cash aid, Medi-Cal, housing help, transportation, job training, and even help with replacing IDs and paying for work tools. The main online doorway to apply for most benefits is BenefitsCal at https://www.benefitscal.com. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)

## Introduction: DPSS Is More Than Food Stamps

Many people think DPSS only does “CalFresh” or food stamps, but California’s social services system covers almost every part of basic survival: food, rent, health care, and work support. These programs are run by the California Department of Social Services (CDSS) and local county welfare departments (DPSS/SSA/HHS/HSA/DHA), and many can be applied for together in one application. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calfresh)

The central benefits portal for most counties is **BenefitsCal** (https://benefitscal.com), which lets you apply for CalFresh, CalWORKs, Medi-Cal, and often General Relief online, upload proof documents, and check case status. You can also still apply by phone or in person if online access is difficult. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)

## Section 1: CalFresh (Food Benefits)

CalFresh helps low-income individuals and families buy food using an EBT card that works like a debit card at grocery stores, some farmers markets, and certain online retailers. It is available in every county and is open to many workers, families, seniors, students with exceptions, and some immigrants who meet federal and state rules. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh/gross-income.html)

### Eligibility basics and income limits

CalFresh is for people with low income who meet federal income rules and California’s CalFresh guidelines. For most households, monthly **gross income** (before deductions) must be at or below **200% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL)** to qualify under California’s modified categorical eligibility rules. If someone in the household is disqualified due to an intentional program violation, a stricter **130% FPL gross income** standard and resource rules apply. [stgenssa.sccgov](https://stgenssa.sccgov.org/debs/program_handbooks/charts/assets/2CalFresh/CFMonthAllIncomeElig.htm)

Households with an elderly (60+) or disabled member have different income tests: the county may use **net income** limits instead of gross income limits, and some higher income thresholds apply. There is no property limit for most households applying only for CalFresh, though combined CalFresh + CalWORKs may involve resource rules. [venturacounty](https://venturacounty.gov/human-services-agency/main-homepage/how-to-apply-for-calfresh/)

Official CalFresh program page: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calfresh [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calfresh)
Los Angeles CalFresh info: https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)

### Expedited/Emergency CalFresh

If you have very little money and food, you may qualify for **Expedited Service**, which provides CalFresh within 3 days and sometimes the same day after your application is received. LA County, for example, explains that expedited benefits are available if certain tests are met and requires an interview and ID verification, but photo ID is not required. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)

### Restaurant Meals Program (RMP)

California’s **Restaurant Meals Program** lets certain CalFresh recipients buy prepared hot food at participating restaurants using their EBT card. In counties that participate (including Los Angeles), it is usually limited to people who are homeless, elderly (60+), or disabled. You must already receive CalFresh and be coded as RMP-eligible by the county; ask your worker specifically about “Restaurant Meals Program eligibility.” [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)

### How to apply for CalFresh

You can usually apply for CalFresh together with other programs (CalWORKs, Medi-Cal, General Relief) through BenefitsCal. In Los Angeles County, for example, you can apply: [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)

- Online: https://www.benefitscal.com [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)
- By phone: LA DPSS Customer Service Center (CalFresh Apply) at **(866) 613‑3777** (press option 2, then 2 again for CalFresh). [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)
- In person: At a local DPSS district office listed on https://dpss.lacounty.gov under “Office Locations.” [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)

Basic steps (similar in most counties):  

1. **Create a BenefitsCal account** at https://benefitscal.com and start a new application for CalFresh. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)
2. **Answer screening questions** about household size, income, rent, and expenses; you can also mark that you need expedited CalFresh if you are in crisis. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)
3. **Upload or submit documents**: ID, proof of address, pay stubs, rent receipts, and utility bills help show your situation. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh/gross-income.html)
4. **Complete an interview** by phone or in person, normally within 7 days for expedited cases and 30 days for regular cases. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh.html)
5. **Get an EBT card** if approved, usually mailed or available for pickup at your county office. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calfresh)

### Recertification process

CalFresh is not permanent; you must **recertify** periodically (often every 12 or 24 months, depending on the household) and complete periodic reports like SAR‑7 in some counties. You will receive a notice when it is time to recertify and must: [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)

- Complete the recertification form (online via BenefitsCal, by mail, or in person). [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)
- Submit updated income and household information. [stgenssa.sccgov](https://stgenssa.sccgov.org/debs/program_handbooks/charts/assets/2CalFresh/CFMonthAllIncomeElig.htm)
- Attend a recertification interview if required. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh/gross-income.html)

Missing recertification steps is a common reason benefits stop, so open all mail from the county and read notices in your BenefitsCal account. [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)

### Work requirements

In California, most non‑exempt CalFresh household members aged **16–59** must register for work as a condition of eligibility. Federal rules also impose **time‑limited benefits** for some adults without dependents; California uses a statewide 36‑month clock for these time limits, and there are exemptions and good cause rules. Many people are exempt due to disability, pregnancy, caring for a child, or other reasons, so always ask your worker if you can be exempt. [wclp](https://wclp.org/calfresh-work-reporting/)

## Section 2: CalWORKs (Cash Aid + Much More)

CalWORKs is California’s main cash assistance and work-support program for families with children. It provides monthly cash, employment services, and many supportive services like housing help, transportation, and child care. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/cash/calworks.html)

Official CalWORKs site: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)
LA County CalWORKs: https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/cash/calworks.html [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/cash/calworks.html)

### Basic eligibility

CalWORKs serves families with at least one child under 18 (or under 19 if graduating by their 19th birthday) living in the home, and the parent/caretaker must live in California and meet immigration and other eligibility rules. Families must have low income and limited resources; as an example, statewide rules set a resource limit around $12,552 (higher if the family includes an older adult or someone with a disability), with exemptions for things like a home and one car up to a certain value. Exact income and grant amounts depend on your county, household size, and situation. [ca.db101](https://ca.db101.org/ca/programs/income_support/calworks/program2.htm)

### Monthly cash assistance

CalWORKs gives monthly cash aid to help pay for basic needs like housing, utilities, and clothing. Payments are generally made through EBT or direct deposit, and amounts are based on a “Maximum Aid Payment” schedule that varies by county and family size. [ca.db101](https://ca.db101.org/ca/programs/income_support/calworks/program2.htm)

### Welfare-to-Work (WTW) / GAIN

Most non‑exempt adults on CalWORKs must participate in Welfare‑to‑Work (WTW) activities, called **GAIN** (Greater Avenues for Independence) in Los Angeles County. GAIN offers job club, vocational assessment, education and training, subsidized employment, community service, and post‑employment services. The program can also help with **transportation, child care, and special job‑related expenses such as uniforms and tools**, and can connect you to domestic violence, mental health, and substance use disorder services. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)

LA GAIN information: https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/jobs/gain.html [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/jobs/gain.html)

### Housing Support Program (HSP) and Homeless Assistance

The **CalWORKs Housing Support Program (HSP)** provides housing-focused case management and financial assistance to eligible CalWORKs families who are homeless or at risk of homelessness. HSP can help with interim housing (including motels), rental assistance, security deposits, and housing navigation; details vary by county and are administered locally through county welfare departments. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)

CalWORKs **Homeless Assistance (HA)** includes temporary and permanent assistance for eligible CalWORKs families who are homeless. Temporary HA can help pay for up to 16 days of shelter (and may be issued in a lump sum when homelessness is caused by a declared disaster), while permanent HA can help with move‑in costs like deposits when securing housing. Disaster‑related homelessness may allow HA to be issued more than once in a 12‑month period. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)

### Transportation, work clothing, and other supports

Supportive services in WTW/GAIN can include:  

- **Transportation** (bus passes, gas cards, mileage reimbursement) to attend approved activities. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gDlgv0amqI)
- **Child care** so you can attend training, job search, or work. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)
- **Special job‑related expenses** such as tools, uniforms, and licensing fees required for employment. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gDlgv0amqI)

These supports are **not automatic**; you must tell your worker what you need for your approved activity or job and keep receipts when required. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)

### Child care and moving assistance

CalWORKs participants may qualify for subsidized child care through the CalWORKs child care program, which is administered by county welfare departments and child care agencies. Housing-related supports like moving costs, security deposits, and utility payments may be available under CalWORKs HSP or Homeless Assistance and, in some counties, under housing programs like HDAP or Bringing Families Home. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

### Emergency payments and disaster-related help

CalWORKs can provide **Immediate Need** payments and **nonrecurring special needs payments** when families face emergencies such as fires or other disasters that damage or destroy housing or belongings. Nonrecurring special needs funds (up to a set maximum per incident) can help with temporary shelter, repair or replacement of clothing and household items, and related expenses. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)

### How to apply and what documents you need

You can apply for CalWORKs:

- Online: https://www.benefitscal.com (select cash aid / CalWORKs). [ca.db101](https://ca.db101.org/ca/programs/income_support/calworks/program2.htm)
- By contacting your county social services agency; use the CDSS county office list at https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices)
- In LA County: https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/cash/calworks.html or call (866) 613‑3777. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/cash/calworks.html)

You typically must:

1. Submit an initial application with basic information. [ca.db101](https://ca.db101.org/ca/programs/income_support/calworks/program2.htm)
2. Attend an eligibility interview (phone or in-person) where the worker asks more detailed questions. [ha.saccounty](https://ha.saccounty.gov/benefits/CalWORKs/Documents/CalWORKs%20Fact%20Sheet%20January%202025.pdf)
3. Provide **verification** such as ID, Social Security numbers (if available), birth certificates, proof of residence, rent receipts or lease, utility bills, and proof of income and resources. [ha.saccounty](https://ha.saccounty.gov/benefits/CalWORKs/Documents/CalWORKs%20Fact%20Sheet%20January%202025.pdf)

Ask the worker about **CalWORKs Housing Support Program, Homeless Assistance, and Welfare‑to‑Work supportive services** during your interview, so they screen you for all possible help. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

## Section 3: General Relief / General Assistance (GR/GA)

**General Relief (GR)** or **General Assistance (GA)** is a county‑funded cash aid program for adults with little or no income who do **not** have minor children and are not eligible for other cash aid programs. Each county sets its own payment levels, eligibility rules, and work requirements, so you must check your county’s website or call directly. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/calfresh/contact-numbers-and-information)

In Los Angeles County, GR is described as cash assistance for indigent adults without dependent children who are ineligible for federal or state programs. Many counties require participation in a **work program**, like LA County’s **GROW (General Relief Opportunities for Work)**, unless you are exempt due to disability or other reasons. Some counties treat GR as a **loan** that must be repaid if you later receive SSI or certain other benefits. [circaworks](https://circaworks.com/ask-the-expert/gain-grow-programs-in-los-angeles-county/)

To find your county’s GR/GA program details, visit the CDSS county office page: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices and click your county to get the local DPSS/SSA/HHS/HSA website and contact numbers. You can usually apply for GR/GA through BenefitsCal or by applying directly through your county social services office. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices)

## Section 4: Medi-Cal & CalAIM Enhanced Services

**Medi-Cal** is California’s Medicaid program, which provides free or low‑cost health coverage for low‑income adults, children, seniors, people with disabilities, pregnant individuals, and some others. Counties help process Medi-Cal applications, and many people apply through the same BenefitsCal portal used for CalFresh and CalWORKs. [ssa.ocgov](https://ssa.ocgov.com/Changes-Medi-cal-Calfresh)

### Medi-Cal basics and 2026 changes

Medi-Cal eligibility is based on income, household size, and other factors, with many people qualifying under the Affordable Care Act “expansion” income rules. Starting **January 1, 2026**, asset/resource limits were reinstated for certain Medi-Cal groups such as adults 65+, people with disabilities, and some families whose income exceeds certain tax-based limits; countable assets can include bank accounts, cash, and some properties. [ssa.ocgov](https://ssa.ocgov.com/Changes-Medi-cal-Calfresh)

Program updates and renewal guidance: “Keep Your Benefits & Stay Healthy” at Los Angeles County: https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/ [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)

### CalAIM: Enhanced Care Management and Community Supports

California’s **CalAIM** initiative is transforming Medi-Cal to better serve people with complex needs through **Enhanced Care Management (ECM)** and **Community Supports** (also called In Lieu of Services). [dhcs.ca](https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/Pages/ECMandILOS.aspx)

Official CalAIM ECM & Community Supports page: https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/Pages/ECMandILOS.aspx [dhcs.ca](https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/Pages/ECMandILOS.aspx)
Community Supports list: https://calaim.dhcs.ca.gov/pages/community-supports [calaim.dhcs.ca](https://calaim.dhcs.ca.gov/pages/community-supports)

**Enhanced Care Management (ECM)** provides intensive care coordination for certain Medi-Cal members, such as people experiencing homelessness, high utilizers of emergency rooms, people with severe mental illness or substance use disorders, and others with complex needs. ECM helps with outreach and engagement, care planning, coordinating health and social services, and supporting transitions between settings. [nashp](https://nashp.org/calaim-leveraging-medicaid-managed-care-for-housing-and-homelessness-supports/)

**Community Supports** can include housing‑related services and other supports, depending on your Medi-Cal managed care plan and county. Examples available through many plans include: [providers.anthem](https://providers.anthem.com/california-provider/patient-care/calaim)

- Housing transition navigation services. [providers.anthem](https://providers.anthem.com/california-provider/patient-care/calaim)
- Housing deposits and transitional rent. [calaim.dhcs.ca](https://calaim.dhcs.ca.gov/pages/community-supports)
- Housing tenancy and sustaining services. [caloptima](https://www.caloptima.org/en/community-impact/calaim)
- Short‑term post‑hospitalization housing and recuperative care (medical respite). [providerlibrary.healthnetcalifornia](https://providerlibrary.healthnetcalifornia.com/medi-cal/provider-manual/calaim/community-supports.html)
- Respite services and other community‑based supports. [providerlibrary.healthnetcalifornia](https://providerlibrary.healthnetcalifornia.com/medi-cal/provider-manual/calaim/community-supports.html)

To access ECM or Community Supports, you must be enrolled in a Medi-Cal managed care plan that offers them and meet the population-of-focus criteria; your plan, county, or provider can refer you. [nashp](https://nashp.org/calaim-leveraging-medicaid-managed-care-for-housing-and-homelessness-supports/)

### Transportation to medical appointments

Medi-Cal managed care plans must provide **non-emergency medical transportation or non-medical transportation** for eligible members to get to covered medical appointments. You can call the number on your Medi-Cal plan card and ask for “transportation benefits” or ask your DPSS worker for help connecting with the plan’s transportation line. [dhcs.ca](https://www.dhcs.ca.gov/Pages/ECMandILOS.aspx)

### How to enroll in Medi-Cal

You can enroll in Medi-Cal:

- Online: Through **BenefitsCal** (https://benefitscal.com) in most counties, or through **Covered California** at https://www.coveredca.com for people who may qualify for both Medi-Cal and Covered California. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)
- By phone or in person: Call your county social services agency; numbers are listed at https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices and on county Medi-Cal pages. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/calfresh/contact-numbers-and-information)

General steps:

1. Submit an application (online, by mail, phone, or in person), selecting Medi-Cal. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)

3. Once approved, select or are assigned a Medi-Cal managed care plan that will coordinate your care and CalAIM services if you qualify. [calaim.dhcs.ca](https://calaim.dhcs.ca.gov/pages/community-supports)

## Section 5: Housing & Homelessness Programs Through DPSS

California and county DPSS agencies operate several housing programs, often coordinated with Continuums of Care and local housing partners. Many of these are **CalWORKs‑linked** or targeted to specific populations. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

Key CDSS housing programs page: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

### CalWORKs Housing Support Program (HSP)

The **CalWORKs Housing Support Program** helps CalWORKs families who are experiencing or at risk of homelessness find and keep permanent housing. HSP provides housing identification, landlord engagement, case management, rental assistance, security deposits, and often motel stays as a bridge to permanent housing; services and amounts vary by county. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

### Homeless Assistance (CalWORKs HA)

As described earlier, **Homeless Assistance** offers temporary shelter payments (up to a set number of days) and permanent assistance for move‑in costs for eligible CalWORKs families. When homelessness is due to a declared disaster, HA can be issued in a lump sum and the usual once‑every‑12‑months limit may not apply. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)

### Housing and Disability Advocacy Program (HDAP)

The **Housing and Disability Advocacy Program (HDAP)** helps people who are experiencing or at risk of homelessness and are likely eligible for disability benefits (like SSI/SSDI). HDAP provides outreach, case management, disability benefits advocacy, and **housing assistance**, including interim shelter, rental assistance, housing navigation, security deposits, utility payments, moving costs, legal services, and credit repair. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

HDAP overview: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

### Bringing Families Home (BFH) and other housing programs

CDSS’s **Bringing Families Home** program focuses on helping families in the child welfare system who are experiencing or at risk of homelessness obtain and maintain housing so that children can remain safely with their families or return home. Other housing initiatives under CDSS include programs for foster youth, people exiting institutions, and other vulnerable populations, and they are administered through county agencies and local partners. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

### Referrals to Coordinated Entry and local shelters

Counties often use a **Coordinated Entry System (CES)** for homelessness services, where individuals and families are assessed and matched with housing resources based on vulnerability and need. DPSS staff may refer you to CES, local shelter access lines, or housing navigators when you identify as homeless or at risk of homelessness during your application or interview. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

Ask your worker directly for:

- “Housing screening” for CalWORKs HSP and Homeless Assistance.  
- A referral to **HDAP** if you are homeless and have a disabling condition or are seeking disability benefits. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)
- A **Coordinated Entry** referral or local shelter hotline for your area. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

## Section 6: Identification & Document Replacement Support

Whether DPSS directly pays for replacement IDs and vital records can vary by county and by program, but some housing and CalWORKs‑linked programs can help with these costs as **supportive services**.  

Under **HDAP**, for example, housing-related financial assistance can include costs needed to secure housing and benefits, and counties may use these funds for fees related to applications for disability benefits and associated documentation. Some CalWORKs programs and local housing initiatives may also cover costs like birth certificates or state IDs when needed to obtain or maintain housing or employment, but policies are local and not uniform. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

Transportation assistance (like bus passes or gas cards) can be provided as a WTW/GAIN supportive service to attend required appointments, including those related to obtaining documents or going to welfare offices. Verification typically involves showing that the expense is related to an approved activity or needed to access benefits, and counties may request receipts or a written explanation. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gDlgv0amqI)

For exact rules in your county, contact your county social services office through https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices and ask whether CalWORKs, HSP, HDAP, or other programs can help pay for replacement birth certificates, California IDs, or Social Security card fees. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/county-offices)

## Section 7: Employment & Training Benefits

DPSS and related programs provide job training and employment support so that families can become more self‑sufficient. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)

### GAIN (Welfare-to-Work for CalWORKs)

The **GAIN** program in Los Angeles County, and equivalent Welfare‑to‑Work programs in other counties, offer employment services for CalWORKs participants. Services include: [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/jobs/gain.html)

- Job Club and job preparation workshops.  
- Vocational assessment to identify skills and barriers.  
- Education and training, including basic education and skills training. [selacowdb](http://www.selacowdb.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/GAIN-Program-Overview-11-2017.pdf)
- Subsidized employment and community service placements. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)
- Post‑employment services to help you keep and advance in your job. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gDlgv0amqI)

GAIN also provides **supportive services** like transportation, child care, and payment for special job‑related expenses such as tools and uniforms. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)

LA GAIN: https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/jobs/gain.html [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/jobs/gain.html)

### Job search support and training/tuition

Through WTW/GAIN, counties can help with job search (resumés, applications, interview skills), supervised job search periods, and referral to training or education programs that can count toward your participation requirements. Some counties partner with community colleges, adult schools, or training providers, and may help with tuition, books, or fees when these are part of an approved activity, though specifics vary. [pathwaystowork.acf](https://pathwaystowork.acf.gov/intervention-detail/591)

Because details on specialized certifications (such as RADT – Registered Alcohol/Drug Technician) and tuition support are county‑specific, ask your GAIN or WTW worker:  

- Whether your desired training or certification can be added to your WTW plan.  
- What costs (tuition, fees, books, licensing exams) the county can cover as supportive services. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)

### Tools, uniforms, licensing fees

Supportive services can pay for certain mandatory work items such as uniforms, tools, safety equipment, and required licensing or certification fees when they are necessary for a job or for an approved WTW activity. This generally requires your worker’s approval and documentation (like a job offer or training requirements). [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gDlgv0amqI)

## Section 8: Special Programs People Don’t Know About

Several important protections and special benefits are built into California’s social services system.  

### Domestic violence waivers and exemptions

CalWORKs/Welfare‑to‑Work has **domestic abuse waivers** and **exemptions** that can temporarily waive program requirements when complying would make it harder or more dangerous for a survivor of domestic violence to participate or work. Counties can waive or modify requirements like work participation, time limits, or child support cooperation when domestic abuse prevents participation; these waivers are granted on a case‑by‑case basis and must be reviewed periodically. Sworn statements by survivors can be sufficient to verify abuse, and documentation is not always required. [socialservices.alamedacountyca](https://socialservices.alamedacountyca.gov/acssa-assets/PDF/CalWORKs-Policies/CalWORKs%20Handbook%2042-7.7%20Domestic%20Violence%20Abuse%20Policies%20and%20Procedures%20for%20Waivers%20and%20Exemptions%20.pdf)

Ask your CalWORKs worker or social worker specifically about **“CalWORKs domestic abuse waivers”** or **“Welfare‑to‑Work exemptions due to domestic violence.”** [ssa.ocgov](https://www.ssa.ocgov.com/sites/ssa/files/import/data/files/32252.pdf)

### Exemptions from work requirements

Beyond domestic violence, CalWORKs and CalFresh provide **good cause** and **exemption** rules for people who cannot participate in work activities due to disability, illness, caregiving responsibilities, or other serious barriers. If you are sanctioned or at risk of sanction, ask for an exemption review and explain your situation in detail. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2024/02/calworks-domestic-abuse-policies/)

### Transportation and child care reimbursements

As noted, WTW/GAIN can pay for transportation to approved activities and may reimburse mileage, gas, or other travel costs when required to participate. CalWORKs child care programs can pay child care providers so you can attend work or required activities, and in some cases reimburse you for child care costs if you meet program rules; specific policies differ by county and stage of child care. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)

### Utility assistance referrals

While DPSS may not directly pay regular utility bills, counties often refer clients to programs such as LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program) or local utility assistance, and CalWORKs/Housing programs may help with **utility arrears or deposits** when needed to secure or keep housing. Ask for utility assistance or referrals during housing screening. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

### Disaster relief and one-time emergency payments

CDSS operates disaster-related programs such as **Disaster CalFresh** and coordinates with FEMA and other agencies when there is a major emergency. CalWORKs families may qualify for nonrecurring special needs payments for emergencies (like fires) and **Immediate Need** payments when they have no money for food, shelter, or essential needs. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/disaster-help-center)

## Section 9: Step-by-Step – How to Access Everything

This roadmap is what many good case managers walk people through.  

1. **Create a BenefitsCal account**  
   - Go to https://www.benefitscal.com and create an account with your email or phone. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)
   - Set up security questions and remember your login; this is where you’ll see messages and upload documents. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)

2. **Submit one application for all programs you might qualify for**  
   - Start a new application and select **CalFresh, CalWORKs, Medi-Cal, and General Relief/Assistance** if you might qualify. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)
   - Answer questions honestly about everyone in your household, income, rent, utilities, and any disabilities or homelessness. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh/gross-income.html)

3. **Schedule and complete your interview**  
   - Watch for calls, texts, mail, or messages on BenefitsCal, and answer calls from numbers you don’t recognize during business hours. [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)
   - If you miss a call, call your county back using the number on your notice (for LA, (866) 613‑3777) and ask to reschedule or complete the interview. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/calfresh/contact-numbers-and-information)

4. **Request a housing screening**  
   - During your interview, clearly say if you are **homeless, couch‑surfing, staying in a car or motel, or at risk of losing housing.** [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)
   - Ask for a **housing screening** and to be evaluated for **CalWORKs Homeless Assistance, CalWORKs Housing Support Program, and HDAP** if you have a disabling condition. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs)

5. **Ask specifically about CalWORKs HSP and Homeless Assistance**  
   - If you are a family with children and applying for or already receiving CalWORKs, insist on being screened for **HSP** and **Homeless Assistance**. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)
   - Ask what documents you need (like a Notice to Pay or Quit, homelessness verification, or motel receipts) and how quickly they can issue assistance. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)

6. **Ask about transportation, child care, and work support**  
   - If you are in a WTW/GAIN activity or working, ask your worker to authorize **transportation and child care** and to cover any required tools, uniforms, or licensing fees. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)
   - Keep receipts and follow the county’s instructions for reimbursements or direct payments to providers. [sgvc](https://sgvc.org/places/dpss-calworks-gain-programs/)

7. **Keep your documentation updated**  
   - Use your BenefitsCal account to upload new pay stubs, rent changes, or household changes as soon as they happen. [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)
   - Respond quickly to requests for verification and keep copies of what you turn in. [dpss.lacounty](https://dpss.lacounty.gov/en/food/calfresh/gross-income.html)

8. **Follow up every 30 days if you’re waiting**  
   - If you are waiting on a decision or housing support, check your BenefitsCal messages and call your county at least once a month to ask about status. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/calfresh/contact-numbers-and-information)
   - If you get a confusing notice, call your worker or the main customer service line right away rather than ignoring it. [ad.lacounty](https://ad.lacounty.gov/about-us/campaigns/hr1-keep-your-benefits-stay-healthy/)

## Section 11: Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Only applying for CalFresh.** Many people qualify for CalWORKs cash aid, Medi-Cal, and housing programs but only apply for food; use BenefitsCal to apply for all programs you might qualify for. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)
- **Not asking about housing programs.** HSP, Homeless Assistance, HDAP, and other housing supports are often only offered when you mention homelessness or housing risk, so always speak up. [reg.summaries](https://reg.summaries.guide/2025/03/calworks-case-processing-during-disasters/)
- **Missing interviews.** If you miss a scheduled interview, your application can be denied or delayed—call back quickly to reschedule. [ca.db101](https://ca.db101.org/ca/programs/income_support/calworks/program2.htm)
- **Ignoring mail or notices.** Important deadlines and recertification notices can come by mail or in your BenefitsCal messages; ignoring them can stop your benefits. [stgenssa.sccgov](https://stgenssa.sccgov.org/debs/program_handbooks/charts/assets/2CalFresh/CFMonthAllIncomeElig.htm)
- **Not updating address or phone.** If your contact info is wrong, you may miss recertification and appointment notices; always update your address and phone in BenefitsCal and with your worker. [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/CommunityHealthInitiativeofOrangeCounty/videos/benefitscal/2098085230604634/)

## Section 12: Timeline Expectations

Timelines can vary by county and by program, but general standards and examples include:  

- **CalFresh application and interviews:** Most CalFresh applications must be processed within **30 days**, with interviews and eligibility decisions made in that period. Expedited cases meeting special criteria can receive benefits within **3 days**. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calfresh)
- **CalWORKs cash aid:** CalWORKs applications also typically follow a 30‑day processing timeframe, though **Immediate Need** payments may be available faster if you have an emergency and appear eligible. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/calworks)
- **Medi-Cal:** Many Medi-Cal applications are decided within 45 days (or within 90 days for disability-based cases), though timelines may vary somewhat by county and case type. [ssa.ocgov](https://ssa.ocgov.com/Changes-Medi-cal-Calfresh)
- **Housing assistance:** CalWORKs Homeless Assistance can be issued quickly once eligibility is verified, including same‑day or lump‑sum payments in disaster situations, but hotel availability and administrative steps may affect timing. HSP and HDAP housing placements can take longer because they depend on finding available units and working with landlords, but they often start with interim shelter or rental assistance while you search. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/housing-and-disability-advocacy-program)

If your emergency is urgent (no food, nowhere to sleep, utilities shut‑off), tell your worker clearly that you have an **emergency** and ask about expedited or immediate help such as Expedited CalFresh, Immediate Needs, Homeless Assistance, or local referrals. [cdss.ca](https://www.cdss.ca.gov/disaster-help-center)
