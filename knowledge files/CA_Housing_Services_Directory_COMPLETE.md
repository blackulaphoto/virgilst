### CalWORKs Homeless Assistance (HA)
**Emergency Shelter & Permanent Housing Payments for CalWORKs Families**

**State Program Contact:**
- **CDSS Website**: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/cdss-programs/housing-programs/calworks-homeless-assistance
- **Fact Sheet**: https://www.cdss.ca.gov/Portals/9/Housing/CalWORKs-Homeless-Assistance-FACTSHEET.pdf
- **Apply Through**: Your County Social Services/Welfare Department

**Two Types of Assistance:**

**1. Temporary HA (THA)** - Emergency Motel/Hotel Vouchers
- **Amount**: $85-$145 per day (based on family size)
- **Duration**: Up to 16 calendar days per 12-month period
- **Eligibility**: CalWORKs recipients OR apparently eligible applicants
- **Special**: Families fleeing domestic violence can receive 32 days total (Expanded Temporary HA)

**2. Permanent HA (PHA)** - Move-In Assistance
- **Covers**: Security deposit + last month's rent OR up to 2 months rent arrearages
- **Requirement**: Monthly rent cannot exceed 80% of total household income
- **Eligibility**: Must currently receive CalWORKs (not just apparently eligible)

**How to Apply:**
- **Not Available on BenefitsCal** - Must apply in person, phone, or mail
- **Form**: CW-42 (Statement of Facts for Homeless Assistance)
- **Contact**: Your county CalWORKs office

## SECTION 4: LEGAL & RIGHTS PROTECTION

### California Civil Rights Department (CRD)
**Enforces Fair Housing Laws & Investigates Discrimination Complaints**

**Primary Contact:**
- **Phone**: 1-800-884-1684 (assistance with complaint filing)
- **TTY**: 1-800-700-2320 or California Relay Service 711
- **Website**: https://calcivilrights.ca.gov
- **Online Complaint System**: https://calcivilrights.ca.gov/complaintprocess (California Civil Rights System - CCRS)

**File a Complaint:**
- **Online**: Create account at CCRS portal (fastest method)
- **Phone**: Call 1-800-884-1684 for assistance
- **Mail/Download Forms**: Available at website

**What They Enforce:**
- Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA)
- Unruh Civil Rights Act
- Ralph Civil Rights Act
- Disabled Persons Act

**Protected Characteristics in Housing:**
- Race, color, national origin, ancestry
- Religion (including religious dress/grooming)
- Sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Marital status
- Disability (mental and physical)
- Source of income (including Section 8 vouchers - California law)
- Age
- Familial status (families with children)
- Genetic information
- Military/veteran status
- Citizenship status

**What Constitutes Housing Discrimination:**
- Refusing to rent or sell
- Different terms/conditions/privileges
- Discriminatory advertising
- Refusing reasonable accommodation for disabilities
- Refusing reasonable modification for disabilities
- Source of income discrimination (e.g., "No Section 8")
- Harassment based on protected characteristic
- Retaliation for asserting fair housing rights

**Timeline:**
- **Deadline to File**: Within 1 year of discriminatory act
- **Investigation Timeline**: CRD has up to 1 year to investigate and potentially file lawsuit

**Process:**
1. Submit Pre-Complaint Inquiry/Intake Form
2. CRD evaluates if complaint falls under their jurisdiction
3. If accepted, CRD prepares formal complaint for your signature
4. Complaint sent to alleged violator
5. Investigation (may include mediation/conciliation)
6. Determination of reasonable cause
7. Potential lawsuit or Right-to-Sue notice

**Offices:**
- Headquarters: Sacramento
- Regional offices in Los Angeles, Oakland, Fresno, and Elk Grove

**Important:**
- CRD (formerly DFEH - Department of Fair Employment and Housing)
- Largest state civil rights agency in the United States
- Provides broader protections than federal law (HUD)

**Alternative: File with HUD**
- **HUD Fair Housing Complaint Line**: 1-800-669-9777
- **Online HUD Complaint**: https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/online-complaint
- **TTY**: 1-800-877-8339

**New Protections Starting 2026:**

1. **Rent Halted During Mandatory Evacuations**: Landlords must stop charging rent during mandatory evacuation periods

2. **Right to Return**: Tenants have legal right to return to units after disaster repairs completed

3. **Lease Termination Without Penalty**: If unit becomes uninhabitable due to disaster, tenants can terminate lease without penalties

**Enforcement:**
- **California Civil Rights Department**: 1-800-884-1684
- **Local Tenant Protection Offices**: Contact via 211 or local housing authority

**Combined with FEMA:**
- Apply for FEMA assistance for temporary housing
- Exercise SB 610 rights for lease protections
- Contact local legal aid (LawHelpCA.org) for advice

## IMPORTANT REMINDERS

**Know Your Rights:**
- You cannot be evicted without a court order
- Source of income discrimination is illegal in California
- You have 5 business days (now 10 court days as of 2025) to respond to eviction lawsuit
- Landlords cannot retaliate for asserting your rights

**Documentation:**
- Keep copies of all notices, leases, and communication
- Document discrimination with dates, names, and details
- Save text messages and emails
- Take photos of housing conditions if relevant

**Don't Wait:**
- Apply for assistance as soon as you know you'll have trouble paying rent
- Contact legal services immediately upon receiving eviction notice
- File discrimination complaints within 1 year

**Language Access:**
- Most services available in multiple languages
- 2-1-1 provides multilingual support
- Request interpreters when accessing services
